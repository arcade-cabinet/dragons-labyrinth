# COMPLETE Level Structure - Dragon's Labyrinth

## THE ACTUAL GAME STRUCTURE
This is NOT a simple 12-level game. This is an epic journey with:
- **3 Major Parts**: Journey TO Labyrinth, Journey FROM Labyrinth, Sealing the Void
- **Dual Path System**: Strength/Harmony (first half), Light/Dark (second half)
- **7 Emotional Stages**: Peace → Unease → Dread → Terror → Despair → Madness → Void
- **Multiple Testing Philosophies**: Based on party composition and moral choices

## THE COMPLETE JOURNEY

### PART 1: JOURNEY TO THE LABYRINTH

#### Act 1: Establishing Identity (Strength vs Harmony)

##### OPENING - The Door Scene
- First-person perspective
- Last moment of peace ever
- Door opens to changed world
- Friend offers to join (first party vs solo choice)

##### PEACE → UNEASE: "What kind of hero are you?"
**STRENGTH PATH: Bandit Cave**
- Traditional combat encounter
- Learn violence has consequences
- Mercenaries respect force
- Sets aggressive tone

**HARMONY PATH: Lost Child**
- Environmental puzzle, no combat
- Learn compassion changes outcomes
- Companions bond through kindness
- Sets cooperative tone

##### UNEASE → DREAD: "How do you lead?"
**STRENGTH PATH: Fighting Pit**
- Tactical combat with permanent consequences
- Command mercenaries, accept losses
- Sacrifice units for victory
- Leadership through fear

**HARMONY PATH: Crossroads Meeting**
- Trust exercises and vulnerability
- Party bonds transcend death
- Share resources and risks
- Leadership through love

##### DREAD → TERROR: "What will you become?"
**STRENGTH PATH: Siege Command**
- Command army against fortress
- Accept brutal costs of war
- Fortress whispers: "dragon protects us all"
- Become feared commander

**HARMONY PATH: Dying Village**
- Save who you can from plague
- Helping spreads corruption to party
- Face limits of compassion
- Become beacon of hope

#### Act 2: Testing Philosophy (Light vs Dark)

##### TERROR → DESPAIR: "Why do you continue?"
**LIGHT PATH: Cleansing Ritual**
- Ancient temple promises purification
- Each cleansing kills to "save"
- Dragon once performed this ritual
- Faith tested by futility

**DARK PATH: Blood Pact**
- Void cultists offer power for sacrifice
- Feed void to gain strength
- Dragon made similar pacts
- Become what you fight

##### DESPAIR → MADNESS: "What breaks you?"
**LIGHT PATH: Last Sanctuary**
- Protect refugees as mind fractures
- Your presence draws void attention
- Can't distinguish real from mimics
- Madness through compassion

**DARK PATH: The Harvesting**
- Trade humanity for power piece by piece
- Harvest others' emotions for strength
- Companions fear what you've become
- Madness through consumption

##### MADNESS → VOID: "What will you sacrifice?"
**LIGHT PATH: Forge of High Elves**
- Hidden elven citadel in peaks
- Companions offer essence willingly
- Test of love and mastery
- Become blessed champion

**DARK PATH: Cursed Forge**
- Volcanic hellscape of first dragonslayer
- Demand blood sacrifice for power
- Test of domination
- Become cursed destroyer

### THE LABYRINTH & DRAGON

#### First-Person Horror Return
- Back to first-person but WRONG
- Procedural generation ensures unique horror
- Sound-driven navigation in darkness
- Dragon as hunter, not boss

#### The Dragon Encounter
- **If Mythic Gear**: Dragon speaks as equal
- **If Legendary**: Dragon acknowledges courage
- **If Lesser**: Dragon dismisses you
- Truth revealed: Dragon was protecting world from void

### PART 2: JOURNEY FROM THE LABYRINTH

#### The World Has Changed
- Dragon's death released the void
- All previous locations corrupted
- Companions need therapy
- NPCs flee or attack on sight

#### Revisiting Each Transition (Corrupted)
- **Bandit Cave/Lost Child**: Now void spawning grounds
- **Fighting Pit/Crossroads**: Survivors beg for death
- **Siege/Village**: Ground zero for void eruption
- **Temple/Cultists**: Overflowing with desperate seekers
- **Sanctuary/Harvesting**: Reality itself unreliable
- **Forges**: Second chance for mythic gear

### PART 3: SEALING THE VOID

#### The Final Mission
- Use everything learned
- All companions matter
- Philosophy determines approach
- Multiple endings based on accumulated identity

## COMPANION VARIATIONS

Each level must account for:

### Solo Player
- Nearly impossible difficulty
- Unique "Lone Wolf" traits
- Different dialogue entirely
- Special endings

### Individual Companions
- **Einar**: Loyal but breaks under pressure
- **Mira**: Optimist who abandons in Dread
- **Sorin**: Scholar, potential traitor
- **Tamara**: Baker's apprentice, innocent
- **Final Companion**: Wise warrior (if needed at forge)

### Mercenary Forces
- No loyalty, only gold
- Can command larger numbers
- Abandon you when things get bad
- Different tactical options

### Mixed Parties
- Complex relationship dynamics
- Synergies and conflicts
- Most narrative depth
- Hardest to manage

## PHILOSOPHY PATH MECHANICS

### Strength Path
- **Tests**: Combat, command, conquest
- **Rewards**: Power, armies, fear
- **Traits**: Butcher, Commander, Tyrant
- **Ending**: Rule through might

### Harmony Path
- **Tests**: Empathy, trust, sacrifice
- **Rewards**: Companions, understanding, love
- **Traits**: Guardian, Healer, Saint
- **Ending**: Unity through bonds

### Light Path
- **Tests**: Faith, protection, sacrifice
- **Rewards**: Miracles, salvation, transcendence
- **Traits**: Paladin, Martyr, Angel
- **Ending**: Become new guardian

### Dark Path
- **Tests**: Ambition, consumption, transformation
- **Rewards**: Power, evolution, dominance
- **Traits**: Predator, Monster, Void-touched
- **Ending**: Become new threat

## EMOTIONAL PROGRESSION PER STAGE

### Peace (0)
- Beautiful world
- Helpful NPCs
- Clear objectives
- Player feels heroic

### Unease (1)
- Something subtly wrong
- NPCs nervous
- Shadows at edges
- Player questions mission

### Dread (2)
- Active corruption visible
- NPCs avoid you
- Companions show stress
- Player realizes scope

### Terror (3)
- Reality fraying
- NPCs hostile or fled
- Companions breaking
- Player desperate

### Despair (4)
- Hope becomes poison
- Nothing works right
- Companions need help
- Player questions sanity

### Madness (5)
- Perspective unreliable
- Can't trust senses
- Companions may be illusions
- Player broken

### Void (6)
- Complete breakdown
- Reality optional
- Alone with truth
- Player transformed
